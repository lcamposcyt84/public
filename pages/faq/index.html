1. Using Amacrux


1.1 How do I buy NFTs?

If we know, buying an NFT is much easier than creating one. Only click on the piece you want to own.The page will show some details: creator, category, description, properties and others.

Depending on the status, the NFT can be purchased at a fixed price by selecting the "Buy now" button. On the other hand, to bid on a timed auction, click “Place a bid” and follow the instructions to know the highest bid, the winner takes the NFT.

We recommend at all times to do your own #DYOR research as this is an informational article only, please do not take this as financial advice. You are responsible for your investment.


1.2 How do I sell NFTs?

Unlike other markets, on Amacrux you can sell NFTs without registration, just navigate to the top right of the page and click the "Sell" button. Select the NFT you would like to sell from your wallet. If you do not have an NFT available to sell, please refer to the "How do I create an NFT?"

Once you import the NFT from your digital wallet and sign the transaction, you'll be taken to the listing page, where you can choose the price and method of sale. You can choose between fixed price (you set the NFT price) or timed auction (you set a start price and reserve with a duration time).

Lastly, you'll see the service fees from the listed at the bottom. Remember that Amacrux includes a service fee of 1.5%, not counting the possible royalties (earnings of the creator).

If you agree, you'll then be asked to confirm your sale by signing a transaction. If you’ve never sold on Amacrux before, you’ll need to initialize your wallet first. In addition, if the item you're listing was not minted on Amacrux but through a custom contract, there may be an additional approval and signature required to allow Amacrux to trade the item on your behalf.


1.3 How do English auctions work?

Auctions are an attractive sales method for collectors who wish to participate in bidding. At the moment, only the English auction is available where the highest bidder takes the NFT. Here, you can set a duration for the auction using the calendar. The default time is UTC. Amacrux will automatically complete the transactions as per auctioneer's instructions. The buyer will need ETH to create a bid. If you're using a Sell to highest bidder auction to sell your item, you can set a reserve price. If you don't receive any bids equal to or greater than your reserve price, the auction will end without a sale. Once an auction starts, you can't change the reserve or opening price.

    2.3 On which blockchain are NFTs minted on Amacrux?


    Initially, NFTs will be able to be minted on the Ethereum blockchain under the ERC-721 (Single) and ERC-1155 (Multiple) standards. Soon, it is expected to integrate the networks of Binance Smart Chain (BSC) and Polygon.


    2.4 What are single and multiple standards?


    There are several frameworks for the creation and issuance of NFTs. The most prominent of these is ERC-721, a standard for the issuance and trading of non-fungible assets on the Ethereum blockchain. A more recent and improved standard is ERC-1155. It allows a single contract to contain both fungible and non-fungible tokens, opening up a whole new range of possibilities. Standardizing the issuance of NFTs allows for a higher degree of interoperability, which ultimately benefits users.

    3. Minting NFT


    3.1 What is gas minting?


    Gas minting are transaction fees paid to miners on Ethereum. Amacrux does not receive these fees and is not able to refund them. Amacrux can estimate gas prices using the ETH Gas Station API, so users can have a better idea of ​​gas fees. Token approval is required whenever you interact with a cryptocurrency for the first time. These are one-time fees per contract or token.


    3.2 What actions need gas fees in Amacrux?


    If you wish to buy, sell or trade NFTs, it is recommended that you have enough ETH or any cryptocurrency available on the market to cover the associated gas fees. You will pay gas fees on Ethereum when:

    1. Approval fee authorizing transactions between NFT smart contract and your wallet (Import case).
    2. Buying and listing NFT.
    3. Listing an auction (Only your first time).
    4. Remove an NFT listing or bids.
    5. Transferring an NFT to someone.
    However, these actions don't require gas fees:
    1. Mark “Gasless” for use of the Delay minting function, available only to create NFTs in Amacrux.
    2. Creating a collection.
    3. Listing an NFT at a fixed price or English auction.
    4. Change the price of an NFT you've listed.


    3.3 What is the “Delay minting” function?


    Actually, the NFT minting process is frozen until the moment of purchase. In other words, it is the buyer who pays the gas minting. Otherwise, your NFT is kept safely stored in IPFS where you have full control. When creating the NFT with the delay minting, you will just need to sign the “minting authorizations” with your wallet. It’s free, and it guarantees that you always remain in control over your creations. The delay minting functionality is currently available for Amacrux default collection only. If you want to burn the delay minted NFT, you will need to pay the gas fee as with the regular NFTs, according to the standard (single or multiple).


    3.4 Is it possible to cancel the process of creating the NFT in full minting?


    If and only if the creator has not confirmed the transaction in MetaMask. Otherwise, the process cannot be interceded until the minting of the NFT is successfully completed; even more so if the information has been uploaded to the blockchain.


    3.5 Where is the NFT information stored?


    Amacrux will store the content of the NFT in an interplanetary file system (IPFS), guaranteeing the decentralization of the data. Note that the blockchain does not allow media files, but the associated NFT will be recorded on the chain.

    5. Troubleshooting


    5.1 What if my wallet is not connecting?


    In most cases, your wallet extension and computer browser might get a little out of sync. There are a number of solutions that could resolve wallet connection troubleshooting like MetaMask:

  • Make sure your browser and wallet extension are fully updated.
  • Clear your cache and restart your browser.
  • Make sure your wallet is set to Ethereum mainnet.
  • Try to connect using a desktop computer browser, such as Chrome or Brave.


    • 5.2 How do I find my transaction hash?


    Holders of NFTs can verify the authenticity of the item by inserting the contract address in the corresponding blockchain explorer. In our case, users will be able to go to the etherscan (Ethereum blockchain explorer) and thus observe the NFT transaction hash. Transaction hashes are unique IDs recording each transaction on the blockchain; this includes NFT purchases, sales, and canceled orders. All gas fees paid will generate a transaction hash.


    5.3 What does a typed signature request look like?


    A signature request means you’re authorizing your wallet to take action, such as connecting to Amacrux’s platform, buying or selling an NFT. If you don't see the sign button at first, don't be worried. In most cases, you will just need to scroll down in the wallet extension window until it appears.


    5.4 Will ERC-1155 NFTs appear in my wallet?


    Many NFTs use the ERC-1155 token standard, including those creations through the Amacrux collection. To view an address's ERC-1155 token transfers, simply head to its tab in the Address page. Clicking on any Transaction Details page that involves ERC-1155 token transfers will show the token IDs & quantities transferred and, where relevant, details of a trade.


    5.5 Why is the wrong owner listed for my NFT?


    When you purchase an NFT on Amacrux, the property may take a while to update. This delay is typically due to congestion on the Ethereum blockchain. It is recommended to have a little patience since at any time the article will be updated in Amacrux. However, if you notice that the transaction has been successful in the blockchain explorer (Etherscan) do not hesitate to contact us to solve the congestion problem.


    5.6 How do I delete an NFT?

You can view the confirmation on Etherscan by searching for your wallet address and checking the transactions tab.
